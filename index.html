📖 ROBO FARM GUIDE ✕

🌾 WELCOME TO ROBO FARM!

Hey there, future farming tycoon! You've landed on a beautiful piece of procedurally-generated wilderness. Your mission: turn this wild land into a thriving automated farm empire. Part Stardew Valley coziness, part Factorio efficiency — it's farming, but with robots.

CONTROLS

W A S D or ↑ ↓ ← → — Move your farmer around the world

1-9 — Select tools from your hotbar

Left Click — Use the selected tool on a tile

E — Open Shop

R — Open Robot Manager

F — Open this guide!

Scroll Wheel — Zoom in/out

Space — Use tool on tile under player

V — Enter/exit the farm car when you're nearby

Shift (while driving) — Handbrake drift. Hold + steer to charge turbo, then release for a speed burst.

ESC — Cancel action / close menus

💡 Pro tip: You don't have to click exactly on tiles — any click near a tile in the game world will use your current tool on the nearest valid target. In the car, watch the drift-charge bar above the hood to time your turbo release.

THE GOAL

There's no real "end" — just an endlessly satisfying farming loop. Plant crops, grow them, harvest them, sell them, buy more robots. Watch your little mechanical farm hands automate everything while you sip virtual coffee and design even better robot programs.

🌱 FARMING GUIDE

THE BASICS

Farming happens in three steps: Till → Plant → Water → Harvest. Each crop has its own growth timer and water requirements.

Step 1 — Till the Soil: Select the Hoe tool (2) and click any grass or flower tile in your farm zone. The ground will turn into rich, dark soil ready for planting!

Step 2 — Plant a Seed: Select any seed from your hotbar (4–8) and click a tilled tile. Your farmer will press the seed into the ground. Make sure you actually have seeds — buy them at the Shop!

Step 3 — Water It: Select the Watering Can (3) and click your planted tile. You'll see the soil darken. Each crop needs watering multiple times throughout its growth cycle.

Step 4 — Harvest: When a crop reaches full size (it'll look bigger and more vibrant!), use the Hand tool (1) and click it. The crops pop into your inventory!

💡 Weather matters: Rain, thunder, and hail water crops automatically, while snow slows growth. Watch the daily forecast and plan robot tasks around it.

CROP GUIDE

🌾 Wheat — The starter crop. Fast-growing, low maintenance. Great for beginners and bulk production.

🥕 Carrot — Mid-tier. Decent value, moderate water needs. Good middle-ground crop.

🌽 Corn — Slow but profitable! Takes time but yields 4 per harvest. Worth the patience.

🌻 Sunflower — Moderate everything. Also looks gorgeous. Sometimes prices spike for these!

🥔 Potato — Your bulk crop. Yields 5 per harvest! Great for robots since they water less.

🍅 Tomato — The high roller. Most expensive crop, most demanding. Perfect for dedicated robot teams.

SEASONS

The year has four seasons: Spring, Summer, Fall, and Winter. In Winter, crops don't grow — so harvest everything before the snow comes! Robots still work in winter, so you can use that time to prepare your farm.

🤖 ROBOT GUIDE

Robots are your best friends. Once you automate your farm, you can focus on expansion, design, and making your robot programs smarter. Early on, follow the Goal Path panel to earn your first robot voucher while you build a stable manual farm.

PLACING ROBOTS

Select the Robot tool (9) and click any walkable tile to place a robot there. The robot will stand by, waiting for instructions. You can have up to 12 robots at once!

ASSIGNING TASKS (NO CODING)

Select the Hand tool (1) and click any robot. The Robot Manager panel will open for that robot. From there:

1. Choose a Behavior from the dropdown (like "Full Cycle" or "Smart Waterer")

2. Set an Assigned Crop (what seeds it should plant)

3. Click Set Area to define the zone it works in — then click the center of your field

4. Done! The robot will get to work immediately.

💡 No coding required! The built-in behaviors handle everything. "Full Cycle" is the most powerful — it harvests, waters, and plants automatically!

ROBOT BATTERY

Robots have batteries. When they run low, they'll return to their starting position to charge. You can see battery levels in the Robot Manager. Upgrade batteries in a future update for longer work cycles!

ROBOT INVENTORY

Robots can carry up to 20 items. When they fill up with harvested crops, they'll drop them at your barn (the starting position). The crops automatically go to your inventory!

⚡ ROBOT PROGRAMMING

This is where Robo Farm gets really fun. Every robot can run custom JavaScript code as its "brain". You can write behaviors in two places:

IN-GAME CODE EDITOR

Click a robot → Robot Manager → scroll down to the Custom JS Code section. Write your behavior code there. It's like writing the body of a function(robot, api).

IN SETTINGS.JS (RECOMMENDED)

Open settings.js in any text editor and find the customBehaviors section. Add your behavior there and it'll show up in the robot's behavior dropdown!

THE ROBOT API

api.pos(robot) — Get current tile position {x, y}

api.findCrop(robot, filter) — Find nearest crop. Filter: { ready, needsWater, type, maxDist, cx, cy }

api.findTile(robot, filter) — Find nearest tile. Filter: { tileType, empty, maxDist }

api.moveTo(robot, x, y) — Path toward tile

api.water(robot, x, y) — Water a crop tile

api.harvest(robot, x, y) — Harvest a ready crop

api.plant(robot, x, y, cropType) — Plant a seed

api.till(robot, x, y) — Till a grass or flower tile

api.idle(robot) — Do nothing this tick

api.mem(robot, key, value?) — Get/set persistent memory between ticks

api.inventory(robot) — Returns { seeds: {...}, crops: {...} }

api.nearby(robot, radius) — Array of nearby tile objects

💡 Tip: Use robot.assignedCrop to get the crop set in the panel, and robot.workArea for {x, y, radius}. Great for making configurable behaviors!

✨ TIPS & TRICKS

EARLY GAME

Start by tilling a 5x5 plot and planting all your starter wheat. Water consistently and sell in batches. Your first objective chain is tuned to reward this manual loop before automation.

Once you earn your first robot voucher, buy and place a "Full Cycle" bot to keep one field alive while you expand manually elsewhere.

MID GAME

Three robots is the sweet spot for early automation: one waterer, one planter, one harvester. Or use three "Full Cycle" bots for maximum efficiency.

Don't sleep on Potatoes — they yield 5 items per harvest and only need watering twice. Excellent robot food!

LATE GAME

With 8+ robots, you can run multiple dedicated crop zones. Try a dedicated Tomato operation — one robot per 3x3 patch, all running Zone Farmer. The income is ridiculous!

Write custom robot behaviors that adapt to market prices. Check api.mem() examples in the settings.js file!

WORLD EXPLORATION

Don't stay in your farm zone! The world has forests, rivers, ponds, and wildflower meadows. There's nothing to collect out there (yet!), but it's beautiful. Take a walk sometimes. 🌲

💡 Secret: Change the seed value in settings.js to get a completely different world layout. Each seed generates unique river paths, forest positions, and terrain features!
